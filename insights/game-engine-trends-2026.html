Frequently Asked Questions

Why are AAA studios switching to Unreal Engine?

AAA studios are switching to Unreal Engine primarily due to the escalating costs of maintaining custom engines ($50-100M+ annually), faster time-to-market with UE5's production-ready features, access to a larger talent pool, and superior cinematic tools like Nanite and Lumen that would take years to develop in-house.

What is the cost of maintaining a custom game engine vs Unreal Engine 5?

Maintaining a custom AAA-capable engine costs $50-100M annually for a 50-100 person engine team. Unreal Engine 5 is free until $1M in revenue, then 5% royalties. For most studios, the break-even point makes UE5 significantly more cost-effective, especially considering built-in features like Nanite, Lumen, and MetaHuman.

What happened to Unity vs Unreal Engine market share?

Unity's controversial 2023 runtime fee announcement accelerated developer migration to Unreal Engine. While Unity remains strong in mobile and indie markets, AAA and mid-size studios increasingly choose Unreal Engine 5 for its superior rendering capabilities, better performance on next-gen hardware, and Epic's developer-friendly licensing.

Which game engine is best for cinematic production in 2026?

Unreal Engine 5 dominates cinematic production with features like Nanite (virtualized geometry), Lumen (global illumination), MetaHuman (digital humans), and robust Sequencer tools. Major film studios including ILM, Weta, and Disney use UE5 for virtual production, and the ecosystem of plugins like Runtime Video Recorder enables professional-grade capture workflows.

What is the future of custom game engines in 2026?

Custom engines will increasingly become niche solutions for studios with very specific technical requirements or those large enough to justify the investment (like Rockstar or Nintendo). Most mid-size and even large AAA studios will adopt Unreal Engine 5, focusing their engineering resources on gameplay innovation rather than engine maintenance.

How long does it take to migrate from a custom engine to UE5?

Full studio migration typically takes 12-24 months, including training, pipeline development, and first project completion. However, studios can begin new projects on UE5 immediately while maintaining legacy games on existing engines. Many studios run both in parallel during transition.
