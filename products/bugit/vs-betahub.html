Key Differences Explained

üéØ Frame-Synced Evidence (BugIt's Unique Advantage)

BugIt doesn't just collect videos and logs separately - it synchronizes everything to the exact frame. When you scrub through the video, you see:

  • Exactly which buttons were pressed at that moment
  • The log entries from that exact frame
  • Frame timing to identify if there was a performance spike

BetaHub collects videos and logs, but they're uploaded separately without frame-level synchronization.

üîí Self-Hosted vs Cloud

BugIt runs entirely on your infrastructure. Your bug reports, videos, and player data never leave your servers. This matters for:

  • Studios with security/compliance requirements
  • Console development under NDA
  • Air-gapped or offline environments
  • Teams that prefer full control over their data

BetaHub is cloud-only, which means easier setup but your data is stored on their servers.

üí¨ Discord Integration (BetaHub's Advantage)

BetaHub excels at collecting feedback from Discord communities. Their bot:

  • Turns Discord messages into structured bug reports
  • Uses AI to detect and merge duplicate reports
  • Provides 24/7 AI-powered player support

BugIt currently doesn't have Discord integration - it's focused on internal QA workflows rather than community feedback collection.

üì± Mobile Recording

BugIt (powered by RVR) records natively on Android and iOS. No external screen recording apps, no performance overhead from capture software - just in-engine recording that works on mobile.

BetaHub has limited mobile support - players need to use external recording methods.

The Bottom Line

BugIt excels at:

Forensic QA analysis with frame-synced evidence. Best for internal QA teams that need to reproduce bugs on the first try, especially for mobile games or studios with security requirements.

BetaHub excels at:

Community-driven feedback collection through Discord. Best for studios with active player communities who want to collect and organize feedback at scale.

Choose based on your primary use case: internal QA vs community feedback.
