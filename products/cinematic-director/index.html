FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What event stream protocols are supported?

The AI Cinematic Director uses a protocol-agnostic event ingestion layer with first-class adapters for UE Sequencer event tracks, DIS PDU (IEEE 1278), HLA RPR-FOM (IEEE 1516), CoT/TAK XML, ROS2 messages, and JSON-over-WebSocket. Adding new adapters is a well-defined extension point — anything that emits timestamped events can be connected.

How does multi-hour coherent output work?

Unlike tools that operate on individual shots, the AI Cinematic Director maintains entity state, camera assignment history, and editorial pacing across the full session duration. Whether your simulation runs for two minutes or two hours, the output maintains consistent camera coverage and editorial logic throughout.

What are event-aligned text overlays?

Text overlays automatically annotate video output with contextual information from the event stream — entity callsigns, simulation timestamps, phase markers, engagement labels, or any metadata carried by the source protocol. Overlays stay precisely synchronized with the underlying events across the full session.

How is this different from Epic's Camera Animation Toolset?

Epic's CAT is an excellent tool for shot-level camera authoring inside Sequencer. The AI Cinematic Director solves a different problem: it ingests event streams from outside Unreal (DIS, HLA, ROS2, etc.), produces multi-hour coherent output across an entire session, and generates event-aligned text overlays. These three capabilities — external ingestion, multi-hour coherence, and event-aligned overlays — are outside CAT's design scope.

Can defense M&S teams use this with existing DIS or HLA simulations?

Yes. Connect your DIS PDU or HLA RPR-FOM federation to the ingestion layer and receive a multi-camera briefing video with entity labels, phase markers, and simulation timestamps burned in — ready for customer review without manual video reconstruction.

What Unreal Engine versions are supported?

Unreal Engine 5.4+. The AI Cinematic Director integrates directly with Sequencer for camera timeline output and pairs with the Runtime Video Recorder for the rendering pipeline.
